MS Dhoni

Born: July 7, 1981

Role: Wicketkeeper-Batsman

> Mahendra Singh Dhoni, fondly known as "Mahi" and "Captain Cool," is more than just a cricketer — he’s a symbol of resilience, leadership, and grace under pressure. Rising from a small town in Jharkhand to becoming one of the most successful captains in cricket history, Dhoni captured the hearts of millions with his fearless batting, sharp wicketkeeping, and unshakable calmness in crunch moments. Under his captaincy, India won major ICC trophies including the 2007 T20 World Cup, 2011 ODI World Cup, and 2013 Champions Trophy. Beyond his achievements, what makes Dhoni truly special is his humility, selflessness, and ability to stay grounded despite unmatched fame. He retired quietly, just like he lived his career — with dignity and class — leaving behind a legacy that will inspire generations to come.
